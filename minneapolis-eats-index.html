Neighborhoods

Northeast (NE)
The city's creative engine. Ex-industrial lofts turned into art galleries, breweries, and the hottest new restaurants. Vinai, Dario, Young Joni, and Hai Hai all call this home. Where chefs take swings.
North Loop
Warehouse District vibes with a polished edge. Spoon and Stable anchors one of the most walkable dining corridors in the Midwest. Lots of pre-game energy near Target Field.
Uptown
Around Lake Bde Maka Ska — indie restaurants, coffee shops, and neighborhood bars. Chilango's lake views are unbeatable in summer. Still a great stretch for casual eats on Hennepin.
South Minneapolis
Where locals live and eat. Myriel, Gia, and Revival are all here. Lower key than NE, but dense with serious neighborhood spots that require zero hype to fill seats nightly.
St. Anthony Main
Across the river from downtown with great skyline views. Historic buildings, a riverfront trail, and Aster House — one of the prettiest dining rooms in the city.
Eat Street (Nicollet Ave)
The most diverse restaurant corridor in the city — Vietnamese, Somali, Mexican, Ethiopian, Korean. Cheap, fast, and legitimately delicious. Stop into Eat Street Crossing for the food hall scene.
